DIY Genetics · Handbook

How to turn raw DNA into your own genetic report

This app runs a whole-genome-sequencing pipeline on your own hardware — from raw sequencer reads to health-variant, ancestry, and 23andMe-style output. Here's what it does and how to run it.

How it's put together

The window you click in is a thin desktop app. The real work happens in a Linux environment (WSL2) behind it, where your GPU does the heavy lifting.

Desktop app → WSL2 backend → your GPU + the reference datasets

What the pipeline does

Your paired sequencing files go in one end; three kinds of report come out the other. On a GPU, alignment and variant calling take minutes rather than most of a day.

One VCF, three reports — health, ancestry, and a 23andMe-style file

Running it, step by step

  1. Set up the backend — once

    On the workstation, run the WSL2 setup (installs Docker + the GPU toolkit + the bioinformatics tools), then download the reference datasets. The References panel in the app shows this filling in; you're ready when the core rows are green.

  2. Get your sequencing data

    You need paired-end FASTQ files (*_R1.fastq.gz and *_R2.fastq.gz). See Where to get real data below. Drop them on the big drive, e.g. /mnt/d/diy-genetics/data/.

  3. Configure the run

    In Run configuration, set the Sample ID, point FASTQ R1/R2 at your files, and pick a caller — Parabricks (GPU) is fastest. Choose whether to include the 23andMe export.

  4. Run — and watch it work

    Hit Run pipeline (or Dry run first to preview the plan). The live log streams each stage; the GPU meter shows the card working.

  5. Read your results

    When it finishes, the Results panel shows your ancestry bars and a table of ClinVar-annotated variants, and offers the health report, ancestry, and 23andMe files to download.

Where to get real data

Two paths: sequence your own DNA, or use a public human genome to try the full pipeline.

Getting your own sample — the saliva-kit route

The everyday way to get your own raw DNA: no needles, no lab visit — you spit in a tube and mail it back.

  1. Order a whole-genome kit

    Buy a 30× WGS kit online (Dante Labs, Sequencing.com, or similar). “30×” means each base is read ~30 times — enough for reliable whole-genome calls. Crucially, pick a vendor that lets you download the raw FASTQ/BAM, not just a report.

  2. Register the kit

    When it arrives, activate the tube's barcode on the vendor's site so your sample is tied to your account.

  3. Give a saliva sample

    Don't eat, drink, or smoke for ~30 minutes first. Then spit into the tube up to the fill line (it takes a couple of minutes), close the cap to release the stabilizing fluid, and shake.

  4. Mail it back

    Seal it in the prepaid return box and drop it in the post. The kit is shelf-stable, so timing isn't critical.

  5. Wait for sequencing

    The lab extracts DNA from cells in your saliva and sequences it. Turnaround is typically 5–8 weeks for whole-genome.

  6. Download your raw files

    From the vendor portal, download your FASTQ (or BAM). Put them at /mnt/d/diy-genetics/data/ and run the pipeline — this is the moment the whole app exists for.

Vendor options

Consumer WGS kits paid

Order a 30× whole-genome kit (e.g. Dante Labs, Sequencing.com), spit in a tube, and download your raw FASTQ/BAM weeks later. Confirm the vendor lets you download the complete raw files — that's what this pipeline needs.

Nanopore, at home paid

An Oxford Nanopore MinION sequences DNA yourself, but library prep and coverage make it an advanced project. Most people start with a mail-in kit.

Public genomes — free, to test the pipeline

Genome in a Bottle free

NIST's reference samples (HG001/NA12878, HG002…) with gold-standard truth sets — ideal for checking accuracy. Real 30× human FASTQ, openly hosted.

  • Search “GIAB HG002 NIST FASTQ”

1000 Genomes free

High-coverage WGS for thousands of individuals, downloadable as CRAM/FASTQ from the IGSR / ENA. Pick any sample ID.

Personal Genome Project free

Participants publicly released their own data (open consent). Some have whole genomes, not just chip data. A free login is required to download.

◆
Quick smoke test: a small chromosome-subset FASTQ (e.g. chr20) runs the whole pipeline in minutes — good for confirming everything works before committing a full 30× genome that takes longer and needs ~220 GB of working space.

Before you interpret anything

⚠️
This is not medical advice. Variants flagged “pathogenic” from raw sequencing are frequently misleading without clinical context and confirmatory testing — they are not diagnoses. Decide before you run the health annotation how you'll handle an unexpected finding, and take anything significant to a genetic counselor. Ancestry percentages are directional only: the public 1000 Genomes panel (2,504 people) can't match a proprietary database of millions.
